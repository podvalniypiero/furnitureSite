TAROT

Tarot cards are a symbolic system that connects to the world of magic with the world of our unconscious archetypes as described by Carl Gustav Jung. The Tarot system as we know it consists of 78 cards. The list of Tarot cards is published in many large groups by name - these are the so-called Major and Minor Arcana.

Of these, 16 - court cards "live" 22 life scenarios, and between them there are 40 types of actions. All cards are divided by elements - 4 elements are equal to 4 suits in a deck of Tarot cards.
Speaking about the elements of Tarot, we mean that the same elements of the elements are in us. This is our matrix structure.

The matrix structure is a given scenario, a frame where we find ourselves when fate makes a new turn.

The court tarot cards are Knight, Queen, Prince, Princess. They are divided into 4 suits, each of which corresponds to one of the four internal elements of a person. We have all 4 elements, but there is a leading element, friendly, neutral and hostile.
Fire, Water, Air, Earth - personify four personal types depending on the inner nature of a person. The leading element is the embodiment of the essence of man in key natural phenomena. The element of man is constant from birth to death. It is a psychological matrix on which other qualities are strung. Throughout our lives, we develop traits of other elements, but the psychological basis remains.

The four elements represent the four personality types. Having determined the type of a person, we can understand the life scenario, predict behavior patterns, attitude to life, actions and reactions in critical situations.

Wands are fire. They describe the volitional dimension. This is the suit of active, creative, strong people who are determined to fight and overcome. The main position is "I want!". He longs for success, leadership, responsibility.

Swords are air and the mental dimension. Swords correspond to people having rational thinking and a will to create. Key position: "I know!". Swords are busy searching for truth, the desire for novelty.
